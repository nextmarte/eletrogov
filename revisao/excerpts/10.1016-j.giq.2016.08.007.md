## Excerpts (full-text via scite)

> "The focus in digital divide research has shifted away from a focus on first-level effects (an access divide) towards a focus on second-level effects (a skills divide) (Van Deursen & Van Dijk, 2011). As such, usage has also become a digital divide object of research. This way, eGovernment channel choice-related research and digital divide research, when conducted in the public domain, increasingly focus on the same concerns: adoption and use of electronic government channels."

> "Belanger and Carter (2009) operationalized 'skills' in terms of experience with several different activities on the Internet, such as the extent to which someone uses the Internet for online purchases. They found that internet usage and online information search experience are significant predictors of the intention to use the Internet."

> "Van Dijk and Van Deursen (2014) showed that nowadays digital skills are a key part of digital inclusion. Other researchers point out that skills are an important determinant in the take-up of eGovernment (Belanger & Carter, 2009). Therefore, it was rather surprising to find that in our study digital skills do not influence channel choice at all."

> "But what about the effects of eGovernment usage, are these also becoming irrelevant in the face of the digital divide? We think not. Because our study also showed that digital skills do influence satisfaction of online channel usage. ... the impact of the digital divide on eGovernment is expanding from channel choice to channel usage."

## Achado principal

Em países desenvolvidos, o **divide de habilidades** (second-level digital divide) é mais relevante que o **divide de acesso** (first-level). Habilidades digitais NÃO predizem channel choice (a partir do momento que há acesso, mais ou menos todo mundo escolhe online quando faz sentido), MAS predizem **uso e satisfação** após adoção. Adoção é insuficiente; **intensidade e qualidade do uso** importam.

## Como sustenta o nosso qmd

- Fundamenta a expansão do modelo Vargas (2021) com variáveis de **uso digital intenso** (C8_*, J2_*, C10_*, C11_*) — alinhado com a passagem "channel choice → channel usage".
- Sustenta a inclusão das **habilidades digitais I1A_*** como validação do "skills divide" como segundo determinante.
- Cita Belanger & Carter (2009) operacionalizando skills via uso de internet para compras (alinhado com nosso H2 e-commerce).
